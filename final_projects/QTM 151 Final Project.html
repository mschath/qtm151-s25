Overview:

The Human Rights Measurement Intiative (HRMI) provides data-driven metrics to assess countries' performances on various human right indicators. The dataset spans from 2017 to present, covering country-level observations. The dataset assesses three types of rights:

  • CPR: Civil & Political Rights (e.g., freedom of expression, protection from torture)
  • PaR: People at Risk (identifies vulnerable populations per right)
  • ESR: Economic & Social Rights (e.g., education, health, housing, work)

Our project aims to explore global, regional, and cross-domain patterns in human rights using HRMI data to identify trends, disparities, and correlations.

This graph shows changes in average Quality of Life scores from 2017 to 2021, comparing high-income countries (HiY) (orange line) with low and middle-income countries (LMY) (blue line). The shaded area marks the COVID-19 period, during which quality of life declined, particularly in high-income countries.

Both groups improved steadily until 2020, but saw a drop in 2021likely due to global challenges like the pandemic. HiY countries consistently had higher scores, while LMY countries made smaller but steady gains.

This suggests that while the world has made progress in quality of life, it hasnt been equal across income groups. To better understand these changes, well now look at annual growth rates to see how fast QoL is improving and whether lower-income countries are catching up.

Insight 3: Do Strong Civil and Political Rights Go Hand-in-Hand with Economic and Social Rights?

Civil and political rights are often viewed as foundational to broader human development, but are they accompanied by strong economic and social outcomes? This raises a critical question: Do Strong Civil and Political Rights Go Hand-in-Hand with Economic and Social Rights?

Here, we are exploring the same ESR (Economic and Social Rights) and CPR (Civil and Political Rights) datasets from before, but this time we are looking at the specific relationship between the ESR and CSR scores.

First, let us prepare the datasets for analysis and merge them together.
