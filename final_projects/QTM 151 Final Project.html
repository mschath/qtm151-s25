Overview:¶

The Human Rights Measurement Intiative (HRMI) provides data-driven metrics to assess countries' performances on various human right indicators. The dataset spans from 2017 to present, covering country-level observations. The dataset assesses three types of rights:

  • CPR: Civil & Political Rights (e.g., freedom of expression, protection from torture)
  • PaR: People at Risk (identifies vulnerable populations per right)
  • ESR: Economic & Social Rights (e.g., education, health, housing, work)

Our project aims to explore global, regional, and cross-domain patterns in human rights using HRMI data to identify trends, disparities, and correlations.
